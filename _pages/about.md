Dr. Rahul Sharma is an academic leader, researcher, and institutional strategist with over eighteen years of experience in higher education, research leadership, and university development across Europe and India. His work lies at the intersection of academic leadership, technological innovation, institutional governance, and global academic collaboration, with a strong focus on building research-intensive and future-ready engineering institutions.

Currently serving as a Senior Researcher at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Germany, Dr. Sharma contributes to large-scale European research programmes in Artificial Intelligence, cyber-physical systems, and intelligent data systems. His research leadership spans interdisciplinary domains including machine learning, algorithmic fairness, and trustworthy AI, with a growing emphasis on AI-enabled software engineering and digital systems integration.

Dr. Sharma brings a unique blend of academic and enterprise experience. His professional engagement with Nordea Bank (Finland) provided exposure to enterprise-scale governance, regulatory compliance, and AI-driven analytics within a cross-border European financial ecosystem. This experience strengthened his expertise in data-driven decision systems, digital transformation, and institutional accountability—capabilities increasingly critical for modern university leadership.

In his academic leadership roles in India, including Professor and Head of Department and Dean of Innovation and International Affairs, Dr. Sharma led major institutional transformation initiatives. He established interdisciplinary research clusters and developed structured innovation ecosystems such as the IDEA Lab, resulting in over \textbf{450 patent filings within two years}. His work significantly strengthened technology commercialization, startup incubation, and industry-academia collaboration, positioning the institution as a national leader in innovation-driven education.

Dr. Sharma has also been actively involved in advising universities in India and Europe on digital education strategy, Open and Distance Learning (ODL) programs, and technology-enabled teaching models. His consulting engagements include collaborations with IU International University (Germany), German University of Digital Science, Amity University, Manipal University, and GLA University, where he contributed to the design and scaling of online academic programs aligned with NEP 2020 and UGC regulations.

His academic contributions include participation in research projects with cumulative funding exceeding \textbf{€15 million}, publications in leading journals and conferences, and supervision of doctoral and postgraduate students across multiple institutions. He maintains strong international academic partnerships across Europe and Asia, supporting joint research, faculty mobility, and global knowledge exchange.

Dr. Sharma’s leadership philosophy emphasizes the integration of research excellence, digital transformation, global engagement, and sustainable institutional growth. He is particularly committed to aligning engineering education with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), promoting responsible innovation, ethical AI, and technology-driven solutions to global challenges.

Through his work, Dr. Sharma continues to contribute to the development of globally competitive, innovation-driven academic institutions that combine academic rigor, technological advancement, and societal impact.
